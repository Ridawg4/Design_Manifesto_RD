Grand Slam Grill Mobile Ordering Redesign

Contextual Inquiry Sketching Heuristic Evaluation Thinking Aloud Figma

Problem: The original Grand Slam Grill website was difficult to use on mobile, with unclear hierarchy, inconsistent spacing, and poor discoverability for ordering actions.

Goal: Design a mobile-first ordering experience that is visually clear, efficient, and aligned with the restaurant’s brand identity.

My Role: Lead designer and researcher; responsible for sketching, prototyping in Figma, user testing, and redesign iteration.

Methods
Contextual analysis of existing site, messy and refined sketching, heuristic evaluation (NN/g), thinking-aloud testing with peers, iterative Figma prototyping
Key Findings
  • Users were unsure where their order was stored during the flow.
  • Primary actions lacked visual hierarchy and consistent placement.
  • Spacing and contrast issues made scanning menus harder on mobile.
Design Decisions
  • Introduced a clearer header hierarchy and unified “Add to Order” buttons.
  • Added a persistent bottom navigation (Home, Menu, Order).
  • Adjusted spacing, alignment, and contrast using Szafir’s perceptual guidance.
Outcome
The redesign reduced confusion, improved task clarity, and shifted the design focus from adding features to supporting real user goals.

Design for Understanding: NFL Ticket Price Visualizations

Data Visualization Five Design-Sheet Process Tableau Vega-Lite

Problem: Raw ticket price data does not clearly communicate league-wide patterns or comparisons without thoughtful visual encoding.

Goal: Create both clear and persuasive visualizations that communicate ticket price differences accurately and memorably.

My Role: Designer and analyst; responsible for sketching, visualization design, implementation, and evaluation.

Methods
Dataset analysis, Five Design-Sheet process, Tableau dashboards, Vega-Lite map design, informal prototype testing
Key Findings
  • Clear visualizations supported accurate comparison but lacked emotional impact.
  • Persuasive visualizations were more memorable but risked exaggeration.
  • Color gradients strongly influenced perception and bias.
Design Decisions
  • Used perceptually uniform gradients informed by Szafir’s research.
  • Separated designs into “Clear” and “Persuasive” sets for different goals.
  • Adjusted low-value color ranges to improve readability.
Outcome
This project highlighted the tradeoff between clarity and persuasion and reinforced the responsibility designers have when framing data.

Design for Collaboration: WSBU 88.3 FM Mobile App

Mobile UX Sketching Usability Testing Figma

Problem: The existing WSBU website did not support mobile listening well and fragmented audio, news, and engagement features.

Goal: Design a mobile-first experience centered on continuous listening and easy content discovery.

My Role: Co-designer and researcher; responsible for sketching, prototyping, and usability testing.

Methods
Competitive analysis, low-fidelity sketching, Figma prototyping, informal usability testing, iterative refinement
Key Findings
  • Users strongly valued uninterrupted audio playback.
  • Navigation clarity improved when audio controls remained persistent.
  • Button size and spacing affected confidence when tapping.
Design Decisions
  • Implemented a persistent mini-player across all screens.
  • Unified visual language across Home, Player, and News screens.
  • Increased tap targets and spacing based on feedback.
Outcome
The final design felt more like a real radio experience and aligned more closely with actual listener behavior.

Design Manifesto

These principles define how I approach design problems and make decisions.

1. Evidence Over Assumptions

Design decisions should be driven by observation, testing, and user feedback rather than personal preference.

2. Clarity Is Essential

Interfaces should communicate purpose and next steps without requiring explanation.

My Design Process

Define
Identify users, goals, and constraints.
Research
Gather evidence through observation.
Prototype
Explore ideas quickly and cheaply.
Evaluate
Test with real users and heuristics.
Iterate
Refine based on findings.
